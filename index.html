Bridging the Gap Between Science and Software to Optimize Processes

I have a background in Engineering Physics from Colorado School of Mines. I chose to study physics because I am driven to understand the root cause and underlying phenomena to make informed decisions about how and why things behave in unique ways. To me, deeply understanding how and why something works gives engineers a better leverage to apply those concepts to optimize processes or to better identify why something isnâ€™t working and debug. While studying physics, I have been able to apply and improve my problem solving skills to various hands-on and research projects at Mines. In addition to my project experience at Mines, I was also able to apply my passion for optical systems and problem solving through an internship at Texas Instruments. I worked in their semiconductor manufacturing as a Photolithography Process Engineer Intern, where I was able to work on three projects simultaneously to optimize and improve the photolithography processes.

Throughout my university projects and internship, I recognized a disconnect between understanding the science and understanding the software processes that drive that science forward. I decided to continue learning software engineering at Holberton Tulsa to be able to bridge the gap between science and software while engineering optimized processes. At Holberton Tulsa, I have been able to gain a technical foundation in low-level programming, higher-level object-oriented programming, and systems engineering/devops. I also have been able to work on hands-on projects and collaborate with peers daily, which has allowed me to see first-hand real-world experience of what it takes to work on a developer team. After finishing the foundational curriculum, I have chosen to specialize in Machine Learning. My favorite projects so far have focused on image analysis, building and training deep learning convolutional neural networks to classify images and start implementing object detection. I will be graduating from Holberton Tulsa in September 2021 and am hoping to join a collaborative team where I can apply my excitement for optics, data analysis, and machine learning to continuously optimize processes.
